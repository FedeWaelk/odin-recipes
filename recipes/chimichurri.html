Chimichurri Sauce Recipe

Green, uncooked sauce for meat.

Chimichurri is an uncooked sauce used as an ingredient in cooking and as a table condiment for grilled meat. Found originally in Argentina and used in Argentinian, Uruguayan, Paraguayan and Brazilian cuisines, it has become widely adopted in most of Latin America.

This sauce may be basted or spooned onto meat as it cooks, or onto the cooked surface of meat as it rests. Chimichurri is often served as an accompaniment to asados (grilled meats). It may be served with grilled steaks or roasted sausages, but also with poultry or fish.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup water
  • 1 tablespoon coarse salt
  • 1 head of garlic, cloves separated and peeled
  • 1 cup fresh parsley
  • 2 cups fresh (or dried) oregano leaves
  • 2 teaspoons crushed red pepper flakes
  • 1/4 cup vinegar
  • 1/2 cup oil

Steps

  1. Prepare the Brine:

    2. Bring the water to a boil in a small saucepan. Add the salt and stir until dissolved. Remove from heat and allow it to cool.

  2. Chop the Ingredients:

    3. Finely chop the garlic and place it in a bowl. Chop the parsley and oregano and add them to the bowl along with the crushed red pepper flakes.

  3. Combine the Mixture:

    4. Add the vinegar to the bowl, stirring the mixture well, then add the oil. Pour in the cooled brine, stir, and transfer the chimichurri to a well-sealed jar. Store in the refrigerator.
