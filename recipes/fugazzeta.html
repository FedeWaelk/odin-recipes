Fugazzeta Pizza Recipe

Description

Fugazzetta is a variation on fugazza in which the cheese is baked in between two pizza crusts (usually media masa), and the onions are placed on top.

Fugazza and its variations are believed to have been invented by a Genoese-Argentine pizza maker named Juan Banchero sometime between 1893 and 1932, who served it out of a pizza shop bearing his name. Banchero's pizza shop continues to sell fugazza to this day in the Buenos Aires neighborhood of La Boca, which historically served as a home to Genovese immigrants to Argentina.

Ingredients

For the dough:

  • 500 grams of all-purpose flour
  • 10 grams of salt
  • 10 grams of sugar
  • 25 grams of fresh yeast or 7 grams of dry yeast
  • 300 ml warm water
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil

For the filling and topping:

  • 500 grams shredded mozzarella cheese
  • 2 large onions, thinly sliced
  • 2 tablespoons dried oregano
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Olive oil for sautéing the onions

Steps

  1. Prepare the Dough:

    2. In a large bowl, mix the flour with salt and sugar. Dissolve the fresh yeast in warm water (if using dry yeast, mix it directly with the flour). Make a well in the center of the flour mixture and add the water with dissolved yeast, along with the 2 tablespoons of olive oil. Mix well until a soft and smooth dough forms. If the dough is too sticky, add a bit more flour.

  2. Knead the Dough:

    3. Knead the dough on a lightly floured surface for about 10 minutes until it is elastic and smooth. Place the dough in a lightly oiled bowl, cover with a damp cloth, and let it rest in a warm place for about 30 minutes or until doubled in size.

  3. Sauté the Onions:

    4. Meanwhile, in a skillet, heat a bit of olive oil over medium heat. Add the sliced onions and cook until golden. Season with salt and pepper to taste.

  4. Assemble the Fugazzeta:

    5. Preheat your oven to 220°C (428°F). Divide the dough into two parts. Roll out one part and place it on a pre-oiled baking sheet. Sprinkle half of the shredded mozzarella over the rolled-out dough. Roll out the second portion of dough, place it over the cheese layer, and press the edges to seal.

  5. Add the Topping:

    6. Spread the sautéed onions over the top layer of dough, covering the entire surface. Sprinkle with dried oregano.

  6. Bake the Fugazzeta:

    7. Bake in the preheated oven for about 20 minutes, or until the crust is golden and the cheese is melted. Remove from the oven and let rest for a few minutes before slicing and serving.
